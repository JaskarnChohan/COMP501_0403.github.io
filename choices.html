Choices

The emergence of artificial intelligence in art presents us with a wide range of possibilities, but it also presents artists, developers and society with a wide range of important choices. These choices are critical to navigating AI art's opportunities and challenges. This section explores some of the key choices surrounding AI art, examining ethical considerations, transparency and accountability, automation and human involvement, societal impact, and the role of education and awareness. By examining these aspects, we understand the choices available to individuals, organizations, and society. We also understand how they shape the world of AI art and its integration into our creative landscape.


Ethical Considerations

Ethics is an essential aspect of AI art, with developers making deliberate choices that address ethical issues. One key choice involves reducing bias and discrimination in AI algorithms that generate art. As Damien Patrick Williams (2022) argues, the choices chosen during AI art development can have a profound effect on its ethical implications. Damien Patrick Williams had researched and found the bias and prejudices within AI art that was being generated due to the datasets. By selecting various and inclusive datasets and utilizing bias-checking tools, developers have the choice of stopping the perpetuation of societal biases in artwork created by AI. Bias and discrimination in AI-generated art is not only a moral issue, but it also influences the quality and societal impact of the artwork. By accepting different perspectives on AI-generated art it provides a more realistic representation of human experiences and encourages creativity. Making choices to address bias and discrimination ensures AI art positively contributes to society. This choice emphasises fairness and openness, ensuring that AI art's ethical implications are consistent with societal norms and forms a more open artistic landscape. Ultimately, developers are faced with crucial choices regarding ethical considerations such as bias and discrimination that contribute to a more inclusive and responsible AI art environment.


Another crucial ethical choice in AI art is around copyright, ownership and attribution. Artists and developers face the challenge of protecting intellectual property rights and ensuring they are properly acknowledged and paid for AI-created art. In AI art, ownership is complicated due to AI algorithms' role in the creative process. AI art is created by combining information gathered over time, so there isn't just one artist for a generated AI art piece. Joanna Zylinska (2020) has proposed that by choosing to honour artists' rights, a sustainable atmosphere can be sustained where creative endeavours are rewarded and creative ideas are embraced to enable AI-created art to thrive. By establishing clear boundaries and licensing procedures, artists and developers can navigate the complex AI art ownership landscape. These choices affect other artists as well as the prospect of AI art, as they form how people view and accept AI-generated artwork in the traditional art world. By making choices that acknowledge copyright, ownership and giving credit where due, creators and developers can shape the AI art community to ensure fairness and morality.


Transparency

The choices around transparency in AI art are extremely important. By choosing to make the decision-making process visible and understandable, developers and artists can understand how AI systems create art. This choice allows people to gain insights into the creative process and fosters more appreciation for the collaboration between human creativity and machine intelligence. Transparent algorithms would help counteract potential issues such as biases, discrimination and opacity in AI-generated art. Kate Crawford (2017), a prominent AI ethics researcher, emphasizes the need for transparency in AI art algorithms. She states that by making the decision-making process public, artists and developers can create a more equitable and responsible environment as people can examine and identify any biases that may arise allowing for necessary adjustments. These choices around transparency encourage creativity and cooperation between developers and artists. It establishes trust within the art community and welcomes AI art into the art world with the assurance of ethical standards and social values being followed.


Automation and Human Involvement

AI-generated art involves major choices about how much automation and human involvement should be utilized in the creative process. Although algorithms can create artwork independently, artists and developers can give input to ensure creative control and uphold artistic authenticity. According to Jon McCormack (2019)'s paper, human involvement is essential as it preserves artistic vision and intention. By choosing to collaborate with AI algorithms as creative tools, artists can harness their computational abilities while bringing their own distinct viewpoints and aesthetic sensibilities to the final artwork. By making this choice, artists can maintain a sense of authorship and give their work its unique creative voice, ensuring that it reflects their own aesthetic vision. Collaborating with AI algorithms as creative tools preserves the artist's role in shaping a piece of artwork. This causes the art world to evolve and incorporate AI into art.


Societal Impact and Education

In AI art, choices are important to determine how they will affect society and respect ethical norms. Developers can make deliberate choices that allow AI art to steer towards positive societal outcomes. This is done by addressing urgent issues and avoiding biases and injustices. As Tracy Harwood (2019) states "Art enhances both physical and mental health wellbeing" which shows the impacts of art on people. Artists can choose to bring attention to concerns like environmental preservation, social justice, or mental health by using AI art as a powerful platform for social commentary and activism which positively contributes to societal change. Simultaneously, choices can be taken to emphasise education and increase public understanding of the benefits, issues and ethical implications of AI art among artists, developers and the general public. Through the provision of materials, workshops, and discussion forums, artists and organizations can help people make informed choices in the field of AI art. By having a greater understanding of AI art it encourages critical and thoughtful engagement with AI art, ensuring that choices align with ethical considerations and societal values. According to Selin Akgun and Christine Greenhow (2021) education and awareness are the foundation of responsible behaviour and the development of an ethical environment for AI art. The landscape of AI art can be collectively shaped by artists, developers and organisations to be a force for good change, one that is in line with ethical principles and benefits the larger community by making conscious decisions that prioritise societal impact, education and responsible engagement.


Summary

Overall, the choices surrounding AI art are crucial in shaping its impact on the artistic community and society as a whole. We can properly manage AI art complexity by using transparent and responsible algorithms, addressing ethical issues, striking a balance between automation and human involvement, addressing social impact, and promoting education and awareness. By making these choices, we can ensure fairness, diversity, and moral behaviour while maximising the potential of AI art to inspire, engage, and contribute to the cultural fabric. This leads to being advantageous to artists, audiences and society by actively participating in AI art decisions.
